# Design System

## Direction

Quiet operator console with dense, predictable surfaces. Use familiar controls, system typography, restrained color, and compact spacing. Avoid decorative panels, nested cards, large hero treatment, and non-standard controls.

## Color

Use tinted neutrals with one blue accent for selection, primary actions, focus rings, and active node states. Semantic colors are reserved for health and failure: green for healthy, amber for warnings, rose for destructive actions.

## Typography

Use the system sans stack. Keep headings compact and reserve larger type for modal titles and page-level labels. Data, form labels, and controls should stay readable at small sizes.

## Components

Buttons, tabs, form fields, badges, panels, modals, progress bars, queue rows, and empty states should share one visual vocabulary. Every interactive control needs hover, focus, disabled, and loading states.

## Motion

Use short transitions and stateful animations only. Agent faces can pulse or talk; loading icons may spin; layout should not animate.
